The Technique behind 3D Printing

Functional Model & Services

FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling)

FDM is a widely used 3D printing method that extrudes thermoplastic filament layer by layer to create objects. This technology is known for its affordability and versatility, making it suitable for various applications. The materials range from standard materials to complex, flexible, composite and high-performance materials. PLA, PETG and ABS are the most popular.

Blend of Materials & Hues

MJM (Multijet Modeling)

MJM is a 3D printing process that uses multiple jets to selectively deposit material (usually photopolymers) onto a build platform. Each layer is cured with UV light, allowing for high-resolution prints with fine details. MJM is ideal for creating precise prototypes and complex models.

For Components with Complex Shapes

SLA (Stereolithography)

SLA utilizes a UV laser to solidify liquid resin layer by layer, creating highly accurate and detailed parts with smooth surface finishes. This technology is ideal for producing prototypes, patterns, and parts with intricate geometries. Because of a huge range of materials, SLA can also be used with biocompatible resins, making it suitable for applications where biocompatibility is essential.

Powder based 3D Printing

SLS (Selective laser sintering)

SLS is a 3D printing process using a laser to fuse powdered materials like nylon into durable, robust parts. It’s ideal for complex geometries without support structures, making it great for end-use parts and functional prototypes. SLS materials offer high strength, heat resistance, and chemical resistance, suitable for demanding applications.

Printing Method

SLM (Selective Laser Melting)

SLM is similar to SLS but specifically used with metal powders. A high-energy laser melts and fuses metal powders layer by layer to create fully dense metal parts. SLM is used for producing high-strength and complex metal components.

Printing Method

BJ (Binder Jetting)

Binder Jetting is a 3D printing technique where a liquid binding agent is selectively deposited onto a powder bed, binding the particles together to form each layer of the object. After printing, the excess powder is removed. Binder Jetting is suitable for creating prototypes, sand molds, and metal parts.
